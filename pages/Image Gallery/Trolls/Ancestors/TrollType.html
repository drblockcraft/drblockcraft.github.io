TrollType, or

A typeface based upon the script used in a popular video game series; in which, all letters have been rotated 180 degrees; the text written in the typeface is read right-to-left; however, the typeface itself is just an alternate to the standard Latin-derived alphabet; its purpose is to look alien, despite being used to render English; this typeface may not be officially endorsed by Her Imperious Condescension; the font supports zodiac symbols, card suites, and emotes; it supports most punctuation; letters of Roman-derived alphabets including Latin Extended A; many other symbols of infrequent use; Arabic numerals; and finally, the font is free for use.

These fonts are inspired by those used in Hivebent - and the rest of Homestuck, a webcomic at MS Paint Adventures. All that's property of Andrew Hussie. There is no "Et. Al." here. Do you even know what "Et. Al." means?

Script used in Homestuck - the troll's Alternian Alphabet - are a variation on the Daedric Alphabet, used in The Elder Scrolls series. Obliviontt.zip on "the Unofficial Elder Scrolls Pages" better matches what appears in those games.

Trolltype was typeset by Sudrien. Some glyphs (the 12 zodiac symbols, and card suites, among other things) are taken from Symbola, designed by George Douros and left open for the world to use. Seriously, you should have it on your 'puter. Thank you to certain folks on the MSPA Forums for their comments and feedback. Thanks to AratnitY and paigefillyr for their input into version 11.

Alternian was designed by Cyanide and is reposted here with her permission.


In samples given in Hivebent, Troll Script is read from right to left (RTL) and bottom to top (BTT), the opposite of Latin-based scripts.

Some of this behavior can be accommodated, depending on the methods available.

Word Processors

RTL: Yes; BTT: No

  1. Install trolltype.ttf
  2. Enable Complex Text Layout (CTL) if needed
  3. Set text to use Right Alignment
  4. Use RIGHT-TO-LEFT OVERRIDE Character to force RTL to start
  5. Use POP DIRECTIONAL FORMATTING Character to force RTL to reset

Websites

RTL: Yes, BTT: Yes, with trolltype-turnwise and CSS transformations

  1. Download trolltype webfont package
  2. use text-align: right; for text alignment
  3. Either:
    • Put &#x202E; At the begining of appropriate paragraphs, or
    • Use CSS to do the same thing :before { content: "\202E"; }

Alternatively, you can include trolltype-webfont.css, trolltype-turnwise-webfont.css, or alternian-webfont.css for basic class-based functionality.
